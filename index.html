Project 1: Interactive Audio & Visual with p5.js

Final Sketch

This project is driven by my passion for raising awareness about Korea’s declining birthrate and aging population, a social issue that deeply concerns me. I wanted to experiment with data visualization for Project 1 while incorporating audio, making the experience both visual and auditory to engage viewers more fully.

The first graph uses bar heights and baby-crying sounds to represent birth numbers, the second shows bouncing population circles whose sound volume changes with their size, and the third visualizes the super-aged population with branching veins filling circles. To tie the experience together, I designed a home page where users can select which dataset to explore, and pressing ESC at any time returns them to this starting point.

Project 2: HyperNarrative

Concept
Final: Kodokushi

This project explores the quiet loneliness behind kodokushi, or “lonely death,” a social issue in Japan where people pass away alone and remain unnoticed. When I lived in Japan as a child, I often saw news reports about it without fully understanding. Years later, learning how this issue has continued to grow made me reflect on isolation, aging, and the loss of connection in modern society.

The work unfolds through slow pacing, muted colors, and minimal design to echo the stillness and emotional distance surrounding kodokushi. Each page moves gently, mirroring the gradual passing of time and the fading of human contact. By combining simple text, empty space, and soft transitions, I wanted to create an experience that asks viewers to pause and feel the weight of quiet moments that too often go unnoticed.
