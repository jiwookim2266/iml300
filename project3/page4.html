Low Birth Rate

Korea’s low birth rate has become a critical demographic concern, with the number of births dropping sharply over recent decades. This decline reflects broader social pressures such as economic burdens, long working hours, rising childcare costs, and shifting cultural expectations that make starting families increasingly difficult. As the birth rate continues to fall, the long-term implications raise uncertainty about the country’s future sustainability.

This visualization translates the decline into shrinking stacks of circles, where each stack represents a year and the number of circles corresponds to the number of births. As time moves forward, the stacks gradually thin out, visually echoing Korea’s demographic contraction. When hovered, the circles turn red and the audio intensifies in proportion to the data.
